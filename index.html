Wall Street ends the week at records ahead of earnings

US stocks capped a winning week on Friday, the S&P 500 up 0.42% to 7,575 and the Nasdaq at a record 26,282, as traders positioned ahead of earnings season. Nvidia rose about 4% and Meta jumped 6% — its best week since 2024. A blockbuster SK Hynix debut added to the AI-fuelled mood.

WHY IT MATTERS
  • Record highs, but breadth still tech-narrow
  • Momentum leans on a few AI names
  • Bank earnings this week are the next test

Yuan hits two-year high as PBOC loosens its grip

China's central bank set its daily reference rate at 6.7989 per dollar — stronger than 6.80 for the first time since 2023 — as a softer dollar gave it room to signal comfort with a firmer currency. The fixing guides where the onshore yuan can trade each day, so a stronger anchor nudges the whole market higher. It marks a shift after years of leaning against yuan strength.

WHY IT MATTERS
  • Eases capital-outflow pressure on China
  • Gives the PBOC more room to cut rates later
  • Supports bank margins & regional currencies
Bloomberg

Circle wins US bank charter; shares surge 14%

Stablecoin issuer Circle secured approval from the Office of the Comptroller of the Currency to operate as a trust bank, letting it directly manage the reserves behind its USDC token, which has more than $73bn in circulation. Its shares jumped about 14% on the news. The charter follows a wave of similar OCC moves from Coinbase, Ripple, Paxos and Fidelity Digital Assets.

WHY IT MATTERS
  • Moves stablecoins inside the regulated banking system
  • Lets issuers rival banks on payments & settlement
  • Blurs the crypto–traditional-finance line
CNBC

SK Hynix soars 13% in blockbuster Nasdaq debut

SK Hynix, the top maker of high-bandwidth AI memory, surged about 13% above its offering price on its Nasdaq debut Friday after a huge share sale. Runaway demand for HBM chips has triggered a global memory shortage and surging prices. The listing gives US investors a direct play on the AI hardware boom.

WHY IT MATTERS
  • Memory is now a key AI bottleneck
  • Hot debut shows investors still paying up
  • A fresh US-listed proxy for the AI cycle
Bloomberg

Hong Kong's ¥500bn offshore-yuan quota takes effect

A doubling of Hong Kong's offshore RMB business funding quota to ¥500bn took effect on 10 July, part of a PBoC–HKMA–SFC package to deepen the city's yuan market. New 9-month, 2- and 3-year tenors let firms borrow yuan for longer to fund investment and trade. The package also flags a planned bond-and-FX trading platform and short-term RMB debt.

WHY IT MATTERS
  • Bigger RMB loan books & fees for HK banks
  • Cements HK's offshore-yuan dominance
  • Advances Beijing's yuan-internationalisation push
