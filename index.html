Beijing supersizes Hong Kong's yuan war chest to ¥500bn

The People's Bank of China and the HKMA more than doubled the offshore RMB business funding quota from ¥200bn to ¥500bn, effective 10 July. The facility also gains new 9-month, 2-year and 3-year tenors, letting companies borrow yuan for longer to fund investment and trade. It sits alongside a package of measures — a 7-day liquidity tender and short-term debt plans — designed to deepen the offshore yuan market.

WHY IT MATTERS
More yuan liquidity flowing through Hong Kong banks means bigger RMB loan books, trade-finance and bond-underwriting fees — and reinforces the city's edge as the dominant offshore yuan clearing centre.

China's central bank set its daily reference rate at 6.7989 per dollar — stronger than 6.80 for the first time since 2023 — as a softer dollar gave it room to signal comfort with a firmer currency. The fixing guides where the onshore yuan can trade each day, so a stronger anchor nudges the whole market higher. It marks a shift after years of leaning against yuan strength.

WHY IT MATTERS
A steadier, stronger yuan eases capital-outflow pressure and hands the PBOC more room to cut rates later — supportive for Chinese bank margins, regional currencies and cross-border flows.
